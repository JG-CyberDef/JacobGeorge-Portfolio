My Story

From Construction Sites to Cybersecurity

My journey into cybersecurity began unexpectedly while working as a Site Engineer. One day, my computer was compromised by a malware attack that resulted in significant data loss. This frustrating experience sparked my curiosity about digital threats and how to prevent them.

What started as a problem to solve became a passion. I immersed myself in learning about malware analysis, network security, and ethical hacking. The more I learned, the more I realized how cybersecurity isn't just about protecting systems—it's about safeguarding people's daily lives, privacy, and livelihoods.

This career change allows me to combine my analytical mindset from engineering with my newfound passion for digital protection. I believe that cybersecurity is one of the most meaningful ways to help others in our increasingly digital world.

When I'm not studying the latest security threats or working on projects, I maintain balance through swimming and cycling. These activities keep both my body and mind sharp—essential for the constant learning required in cybersecurity. For me, professional growth goes hand-in-hand with personal development.
